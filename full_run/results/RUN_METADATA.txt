    --out /home/ubuntu/full_run/results --epochs 200
Models: gcn, arma_gnn, gat, gin, transformer, nnconv (all edge-aware).

--- Known caveats in the numbers ---
* arma_gnn OOD held_out=UK -> NaN (ARMA diverged on that split; a real instability, not a data bug).
* Per-quantity NRMSE for V/theta can be large (>1) for some transfers because V is
  tightly bounded (small target range -> range-normalized error inflates); read P/Q/theta
  alongside V. Aggregate nrmse is the [P,Q,V,theta] combined range-NRMSE.
* Cross-grid transfer to a *single* grid degrades a lot (esp. IEEE118->others);
  OOD (train on 3 grids) generalizes to the held-out grid far better -> multi-grid
  training is the key generalization lever.
